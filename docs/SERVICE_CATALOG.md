# Dental Service Catalog

This catalog outlines the six supported dental treatments Clara can book, detailing duration, preparations, aftercare, and escalation rules.

---

## 1. Dental Cleaning
- **Description**: Professional removal of plaque, tartar, and surface stains, followed by tooth polishing.
- **Approximate Duration**: 45 minutes.
- **Preparation**: Brush and floss teeth before coming in.
- **Aftercare**: Avoid eating or drinking dark-colored items (coffee, tea, red wine) for 30 minutes after treatment.
- **When to Escalate**: Escalate to a human if the user reports severe gum bleeding, mobile teeth, or active infections.

---

## 2. Root Canal
- **Description**: Removal of infected pulp tissue from inside the root of a tooth, followed by cleaning, shaping, and sealing.
- **Approximate Duration**: 60 to 90 minutes.
- **Preparation**: Eat a light meal before the appointment (unless sedation is planned) and take any prescribed medications.
- **Aftercare**: Avoid chewing on the treated tooth until it is fully restored with a crown. Mild soreness is normal for a few days.
- **When to Escalate**: Escalate immediately if the patient reports severe facial swelling, difficulty breathing, or high fever.

---

## 3. Teeth Whitening
- **Description**: In-office chemical bleaching of teeth using professional-grade whitening gel and light activation.
- **Approximate Duration**: 60 minutes.
- **Preparation**: It is highly recommended to have a dental cleaning done within the past 3 months prior to whitening.
- **Aftercare**: Follow the "white diet" (no coffee, tea, soy sauce, chocolate, or berries) for 48 hours to prevent staining.
- **When to Escalate**: None (whitening is a cosmetic elective).

---

## 4. Braces Consultation
- **Description**: Evaluation by our orthodontist to review teeth alignment and recommend traditional braces or clear aligners (Invisalign).
- **Approximate Duration**: 30 minutes.
- **Preparation**: No special preparation is required.
- **Aftercare**: None.
- **When to Escalate**: If the patient is asking specific questions about active orthodontic emergencies (e.g. broken wires poking gums), transfer immediately.

---

## 5. Tooth Extraction
- **Description**: Removal of a severely damaged, decayed, or crowded tooth from its socket in the bone.
- **Approximate Duration**: 45 minutes.
- **Preparation**: Inform the clinic of all active medications (especially blood thinners). Do not smoke on the day of the procedure.
- **Aftercare**: Keep gauze in place for 30 minutes. Avoid hot foods, straws, smoking, and vigorous spitting for 24 hours to prevent dry socket.
- **When to Escalate**: If the patient calls reporting heavy bleeding that won't stop, or severe pain unmanaged by pain relievers post-extraction.

---

## 6. General Consultation
- **Description**: Comprehensive oral examination, screening for oral issues, and discussion of dental treatment plans.
- **Approximate Duration**: 30 minutes.
- **Preparation**: Bring a list of current medications and dental history records.
- **Aftercare**: None.
- **When to Escalate**: None.
